Sports Attractions of New England

Massachusetts

Gillette Stadium is home to the New England Patriots football team featuring a unique lighthouse and a creative bridge as you enter the stadium. The stadium opened in 2002 and can hold over 65,000 people. The New England Patriots have brought 5 Lombardi trophies home to Foxborough. Tom Brady, the quarterback of the Patriots, has spent 18 years with the team.

The Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame is located in Springfield, Massachusetts. This dome-shaped building features the history of basketball, including hall of Framers like Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Bill Russell, Wilt Chamberlain, and more. The Basketball Hall of Fame celebrated its 50th anniversary in 2009 and is continuing to grow through the constant addition of new Hall of Framers. The hall, named after James Naismith, the inventor of basketball, was opened in 1968 and has been a unique sight for all basketball fans.

Connecticut

Gampel Pavilion is home to the UCONN Huskies basketball and volleyball program. This arena is the largest on-campus facility in all of New England the Northeast. This building has held many successful basketball teams as the women's team has been dominant over the years, winning four straight NCAA championships while the men's team has won the NCAA championship in 2011 and 2014.

Rhode Island

McCoy Stadium is the field of the Pawtucket Red Sox, a farm team of the MLB team, Boston Red Sox. The stadium also holds fundraisers and charity events for kids and members of the community. Opened in 1946, McCoy Stadium displays the family-friendly baseball team and creates a fun environment for all.

New Hampshire

Wildcat Stadium is the field of the University of New Hampshire Wildcats. The stadium is not only a resource for the university, but for the entire state of New Hampshire, hosting events like Special Olympics and state competitions.

Vermont

Developed in 2003, this field is used by the Vermont Mountaineers, a baseball team in Vermont that competes in the New England Collegiate Baseball League.

Maine

Hadlock Field is where the Portland Sea Dogs, a Minor League team, play. This stadium is very popular, as high school baseball teams play here as well. Named after Edson Hadlock Jr., a baseball coach from Portland, the stadium features a similar version of Fenway Park's "Green Monster" and a lighthouse in centerfield.
